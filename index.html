the Linked Land Governance Thesaurus

What

LandVoc is a thesaurus covering concepts related to land governance.


LandVoc currently (version 2.0) consists of 310 concepts organized hierarchically and it is available 4 languages (English, French, Spanish and Portuguese) and is continuously translated into more languages (Khmer, Vietnamese, Burmese, Thai, Swahili, Arabic...).

A thesaurus captures the richness of a great variety of terms, because it includes translations, synonyms and relations. This information is consolidated in a unique identifier (URI) for each concept. This is extremely beneficial to index information and retrieve content through browsing and searching.

Use

Why use LandVoc?

Using a thesaurus helps with search and discovery of information. LandVoc enables information providers to index content using their own words and be heard across the world.

Even if a user searches for the same concept using a different term than what was used to index (e.g. 'slums' and 'townships'), LandVoc has consolidated these synonyms or translations. Therefore, it bridges the language and culture gaps that prevent information to adequately flow in the land sector.

How to use LandVoc?

LandVoc can be integrated into repository systems and websites that publish any kind of information related to land governance.

You can also use it to find key phrases related to land governance and their translations and explore the different ways a concept is described in different contexts. It can also be used to connect to and exchange information with other databases with other vocabularies, as LandVoc is integrated within FAO's Agrovoc.
